The ramayana written by Tulsilas Known as Ramcharritmanas is a sixtheenth century epic. The Ramayan is not only a literary masterpiece but also a spiritual guide and a living tradition in Indian culture. It has influenced countless art forms, festivals (like Ram Navami and Diwali), dance dramas (Ramlila), and rituals. Lord Rama is revered as an avatar of Lord Vishnu, and the story reinforces ideals such as righteousness, self-discipline, and devotion. Different Versions and Adaptations Besides the original Sanskrit text by Valmiki, numerous regional versions exist across India and Southeast Asia, such as the Tamil Kamban Ramayanam, the Bengali Krittivasi Ramayan, and the Indonesian Ramayana Kakawin. There are also modern adaptations in literature, television, and theater that continue to keep the epic relevant. Why It Matters Today The Ramayan’s profound ethical and moral messages continue to resonate deeply, providing guidance on personal conduct, leadership, and social responsibility. It remains a foundational text for Hindu philosophy and a source of inspiration for millions globally.
